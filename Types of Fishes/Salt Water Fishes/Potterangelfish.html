Potter's Angelfish


About

  • Scientific Name: Centropyge potteri
  • Family: Pomacanthidae
  • Other Names: Potter Angelfish
  • Minimum Tank Size: 70 gallons
  • Care: Difficult
  • Size: 5 inches
  • Reef Compatible: With caution
  • Temperment: Semi-aggressive
  • Aquarium: Likes to eat live plants, so fake plants are ideal if you do not want your plants eaten. Gentle circulation. While they can live in a small bowl they do best with a larger aquarium with a filter system. They do not need a heater in an indoor aquarium."
  • Water Chemistry: 72-78° F, dKH 8-12, pH 8.1-8.4, sg 1.020-1.025
  • pH: pH 8.1-8.4
  • Color Form: Orange, Purple, Yellow
  • Temperature: 72-78° F
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Origin: Hawaiian Islands
  • Overview: The Potter's Angelfish, or Potter's Pygmy Angelfish, is endemic to the Hawaiian Islands area, including the Johnston Atoll. The body is a brilliant orange, marbleized with pale to dark blue. The caudal portions of the dorsal and anal fins as well as the caudal fin are a dark blue-black. The Potter's Angelfish requires at least a 70 gallon, well-established tank with multiple hiding places and live rock for grazing on microalgae. It may attack soft corals and other sessile invertebrates in the reef aquarium. It is hermaphroditic, difficult to breed, and there are no distinguishing characteristics to help differentiate males from females. The Potter's Angelfish should be offered a variety of foods including Spirulina, marine algae, high-quality angelfish preparations, mysis or frozen shrimp, and other meaty items.
